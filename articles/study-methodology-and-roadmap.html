Study Methodology: Active Recall and Spaced Repetition

Mastering complex subjects through scientifically proven learning techniques at EduHome Tuition Center.

Effective studying isn't about how long you sit with a book, but how you engage with the information. At EduHome Tuition Center, we prioritize two scientifically proven methods: Active Recall and Spaced Repetition.

1. Active Recall

Instead of re-reading a chapter (passive), try to explain the core concepts from memory (active). For example, after studying the structure of an atom, close the book and draw the Bohr model from scratch.

2. Spaced Repetition

Don't "cram" all night before an exam. Reviewing the same topic over increasing intervals (1 day, 1 week, 1 month) moves information from short-term to long-term memory.

Mathematics: The Foundation of Problem Solving

Mathematics is not just about numbers; it's about logic and pattern recognition. Whether you are solving linear equations or calculating the area of a circle, the steps remain consistent:

  1. Analyze: What is given? What is required?
  2. Strategize: Which formula or theorem applies?
  3. Execute: Step-by-step calculation with precision.
  4. Verify: Does the answer make sense?

Science: Understanding the World Around Us

From the chemical reactions in a test tube to the laws of motion governing a falling apple, science is everywhere.

  • Physics: Focus on understanding the derivation of formulas.
  • Chemistry: Master the periodic table and valency to predict reactions.
  • Biology: Use diagrams to visualize complex systems like the human heart or plant cells.
