The Crossroads

Don't Let Peer Pressure Decide Your Child's Future

Every June, thousands of parents in Kollam face the same dilemma: "Which stream should my child pick after Class 10?" Often, the decision is made based on what the child's friends are doing or which stream is perceived as more "prestigious."

At EduHome, we see the results of these forced decisions. A student with a natural flair for Economics struggling in a Physics lab, or a born scientist bored in a Business Studies class. This leads to burnout, low marks, and a loss of confidence that can last a lifetime.

Choosing the right stream is about Aptitude, Interest, and Career Goals. It's not about which path is "better," but which path is right for *your* child. Our mentors in Kottathala are dedicated to helping parents and students navigate this critical transition with clarity and data.

Whether you're aiming for Medicine, Engineering, Civil Services, or Creative Arts, the foundation must be built on the right ground.

🧬

The Science Path

Ideal for those with strong logical reasoning and a curiosity about the natural world. Opens doors to Engineering, Medical, Research, and Pure Sciences.

The Commerce Path

Parental Guide

How to Support Your Child's Choice

Essential steps for parents to take during the stream selection process.

🗣️

"Listen Before You Suggest"

Have an open conversation about what subjects they actually enjoy. A child who loves biology will naturally work harder in it than in a subject they find boring.

📊

"Analyze Aptitude, Not Just Marks"

Marks can be inflated by rote memorization. Aptitude is about how easily they grasp the underlying logic. Our diagnostic tools can help identify this.

🌍

"Research Future Careers"

The job market in 2030 will look very different. We provide insights into emerging fields to help you make a future-proof choice.

🏫

"Choose the Right Environment"

The best stream needs the best environment. Our centre in Kottathala provides the personalized attention required to excel in these tough years.
